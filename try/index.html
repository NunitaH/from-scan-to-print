EINSCAN PRO HD

This brief tutorial is designed to help you kickstart your printing journey with the EinScan Pro HD. Follow these simple steps to set up your scanner and start creating high-quality 3D models. Let’s get started!

Start tutorial

Warning Before Scanning Object:

OBJECT TYPE

To scan objects without problems, it is recommended to scan objects that have sufficient geometric features. Objects with few geometric features, such as flat surfaces, are difficult for feature alignment. In such cases, sticking markers on these areas is suggested to ensure accurate scanning and alignment.

NOT RECOMMENDED

  • Living organisms
  • Transparent objects
  • Sensitive electronic devices
  • Fragile or valuable items
  • Highly reflective and shiny surfaces
  • Explosive or flammable materials
  • Biological or chemical hazards

Requirements:

Minimum Configuration:

  • Processor: Intel® Xeon E3-1230, Intel® i5-3470, Intel® i7-3770
  • Display Card: Nvidia Quadro P1000 or higher; Nvidia GTX660 or higher
  • Memory: 8GB RAM
  • Storage: At least 8GB
  • USB: Minimum one USB 3.0 port
  • Screen Display: 1920×1080 resolution

Recommended Configuration:

  • Processor: i7-8700
  • Display Card: NVIDIA GTX1060 or higher
  • Memory: 32GB or more
  • Storage: 32GB or more
  • USB: At least one USB 3.0 port
  • Screen Display: 1920×1080 resolution

If the computer has multiple graphic card, access to the NVIDIA Control Panel (right click on the desktop). In Manage 3D Settings > Program Settings, Add EXScan software. Then change the preferred graphic processor for this program. Select NVIDIA processor. Click Apply to save the settings.

STEP 1

Connect Power and USB

  1. Connect the scanner power line and adapter. Attach the evasion pod to the scanner.
  2. Connect the adapter and evasion line. Plug the USB end of the aviation line into the computer for the industry path.

Check picture

STEP 2

Scanner Calibration

  1. Ensure the software is installed on your system. If not go to installation. Check how to install software here page 14.
  2. Open the installed software. ->
    • Click on the “Calibrate” option following the on-screen instructions.
  3. Positioning the Scanner:
    • Hold the scanner in your hand with the color camera side facing you.
    • Place the calibration board according to the software guidance.
    • Press the button on the scanner to activate capturing.
  4. Capture Process:
    • Follow the on-screen instructions to move the scanner across the calibration board.
    • Capture all positions indicated by the software, ensuring the symbol turns green.
    • The software will automatically guide you to the next position.

It is necessary to calibrate when:

  1. Device Change
  2. Bumpy Transportation
  3. Decreased Accuracy
  4. Bumpy Transportation
  5. No Calibration for 15 Days or More
  6. Plugged in with Color Camera

The acceptable accuracy after calibration in handheld mode is when the result is less than or equal to 0.05mm. If the deviation is more than 0.05mm, recalibration is recommended.

STEP 3

Scan Mode Selection

Use Fixed Scan without Turntable when:

  • High accuracy and high resolution are required;
  • Suitable for objects that are hard to move or too big for turntable scanning.

Use Handheld Rapid Scan mode when:

  • High accuracy and high-resolution handheld scanning are required;
  • Suitable for objects that are hard to move or too big for turntable scanning.

Use Handheld Rapid Scan mode when:

  • A fast scanning experience is needed;
  • Objects with good geometry for feature alignment;
  • Objects are hard to move or too big to stay on turntable for scanning.

Note: If you have imported a global marker file, new markers cannot be added during the scan.

STEP 4

Handheld Scan

  1. Open the scanning software and create a new project or load an existing one.
  2. Choose the desired scanning accuracy level (medium or low) based on your project requirements.
  3. Markers Placement (Optional):
    • Before scanning, place markers (mock points) regularly on the surface of the object using markers or reference points.
    • Ensure even distribution of mock points to enhance tracking during the scanning process.

STEP 5

Brightness Adjustment

  1. Use the software interface to adjust the brightness. Locate the relevant button and make necessary adjustments.
  2. Double-click the Play button to enter/exit the exposure adjustment menu. Adjust brightness by pressing “+”/”-” buttons on the scanner or dragging the cursor left (-) or right (+).

Proper Brightness Setting:

  • Red indicates overexposed, black is underexposed, and white/light grey is optimal.
  • Ensure the brightness is at a level where lines and markers are clearly visible without being overexposed.

TIPS BEFORE SCAN:

  • Choose a suitable background to minimize noise during scanning.
  • Pay attention to the prompt lights on the scanner to ensure optimal distance.
  • Review the software prompts for guidance on distance and lighting conditions.
  • Practice scanning at a constant speed for better results.

Tracking Loss:

  • In case of tracking loss, return to the previous position where scanning was successful and restart the tracking process.
  • Use the distance prompts on both the scanner and software to ensure the proper scanning distance.

STEP 6

Scanning Process

  1. Double-click the start button on the scanner to begin the scanning process.
  2. Observe the adjustment results in real-time. Clear visibility of matte points and projection lines indicates optimal settings.
  3. Maintain a consistent scanning speed for best results.


Not Recommended During the Scan:

  • Moving the Object: It is not recommended to move the object during the scan to maintain the position relationship between the object and markers.
  • Scanning Objects with Simple Features: Objects with simple features like plain, symmetric surfaces such as spheres or cylinders are not recommended for feature alignment

STEP 7

Post-Processing

  1. Once scanning is complete, click the complete button on the software interface.
  2. Use the software tools for simple edits. Select areas with the left mouse button and perform actions like deletion or addition.

3. Manual Alignment:

  • You can scan several times making different projects one folder.
  • Then you can align models placing markers manually. To combine the data, a minimum of 3 markers are required.

Edit menu

  • SHIFT + Left mouse：Select redundant area, the selected section will be displayed in red.
  • Ctrl + Left mouse: Deselect selected data.
  • Click this button or “DELETE” in the keyboard to delete selected data.

STEP 7

Type of mesh Selection

Watertight Model:

  • Characteristics: Every hole in the model is automatically filled, ensuring a seamless and solid structure.
  • Usage: Suitable for direct 3D printing as the model is complete and closed.
  • Processing Time: Generally quicker than creating an Unwatertight model.

Unwatertight Model:

  • Characteristics: The model remains as scanned, with any unclosed parts preserved in the final model.
  • Usage: Helpful when maintaining the original scanned details without any modifications.
  • Mesh Resolution: Does not require selecting a mesh resolution for a watertight finish.

STEP 8

EXPORT

  1. After software meshed your model, click Save to export the data.
  2. By default saving format is .stl.
  3. Go to the next post to learn more how to print your scanned model.
